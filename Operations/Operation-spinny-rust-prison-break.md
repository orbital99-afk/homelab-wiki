# Operation Spinny Rust Prison Break

## Objective

Liberate Reese from the aging original Apple hard drive, hereafter known as:

**Old Rust Prison**

and migrate operations to a modern SSD before the laws of physics made the decision for us.

---

## Background

For many years Reese operated from the original Apple hard drive supplied with the 2012 Mac mini.

While the drive remained functional, it possessed all the performance characteristics of a heavily sedated tortoise attempting to tow a caravan uphill.

The Committee therefore approved a migration to SSD storage.

---

## Mission Plan

### Target

- 1 TB PNY SATA SSD

### Method

- Live disk clone using `dd`

### Risk Assessment

#### Root

> "It'll be fine."

#### Finch

> "Define fine."

#### Root

> "We'll find out together."

#### Health & Safety

> "Strongly opposed."

---

## Phase 1: The Great Escape

A complete clone of Old Rust Prison was performed.

The SSD was installed.

Reese booted successfully.

Celebrations commenced.

Victory was declared.

This proved to be premature.

---

Following boot from SSD:

## Root Cause

The migration had been performed while services were active.

As a result:

- Portainer database corruption occurred.
- Prometheus TSDB corruption occurred.

This was later classified as:

**An Educational Experience**

---

## Phase 3: The Return of Old Rust Prison

At this point Old Rust Prison had every right to retire.

Instead it volunteered for one final mission.

The original drive was mounted read-only using a USB enclosure.

Recovery operations commenced.

### Portainer Recovery

Database restored successfully.

### Prometheus Recovery

TSDB restored successfully.

### Committee

Mood improved significantly.

---

## Phase 4: Victory

Following restoration:

- Docker operational
- Portainer operational
- Prometheus operational
- Grafana operational
- Zoe (Homepage) operational
- Tailscale operational
- NUT operational
- Uptime Kuma operational

Reese was officially liberated from spinning rust.

## Final Verdict

The SSD migration was ultimately successful.

The operation achieved:

- Faster boot times
- Faster application performance
- Reduced storage latency
- Increased responsiveness
- Significantly fewer mechanical noises suggesting impending doom

---

## Lessons Learned

- "The clone completed successfully" is not the same thing as "the migration is finished."
- Databases have opinions about being copied while running.
- Never underestimate the value of a rollback plan.
- Retired hardware occasionally returns for one final heroic act.
- Celebrating before checking Prometheus is considered poor operational procedure.
